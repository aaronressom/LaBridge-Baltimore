# Welcome to LabBridge Baltimore
## Bridging the "Biotech Wall" through inclusive, accelerated vocational training.

🔬 Project Overview
LabBridge Baltimore is a social enterprise developed for the Social Entrepreneurship Theory and Practice (Spring 2026) course. The project addresses a critical economic and industrial gap in Baltimore: the "Biotech Wall."

While world-class institutions like Johns Hopkins face a constant shortage of lab technicians, many local residents are locked out of these careers due to the requirement of a traditional four-year degree. LabBridge provides an alternative pathway.

🚀 The Solution: A 12-Week Accelerator
Our model bypasses traditional degree barriers through an intensive "Lab-Ready" micro-credentialing program:

Vocational Training: 12-week curriculum focused on high-demand skills like digital microscopy, slide preparation, and laboratory safety.

Social Lab Model: An "earn-while-you-learn" approach where students process overflow laboratory work under supervision, earning a living wage during their training.

Direct Placement: Graduates are directly funneled into entry-level technician roles at partner hospitals and research institutions.

💰 Business Model
LabBridge is designed to be economically self-sustaining and accessible:

B2B Recruitment: Revenue is generated through "success fees" paid by hospitals for each successfully placed technician.

No Student Tuition: By shifting the cost to the employer, we remove the financial barrier for local Baltimore residents.

Operational Revenue: The Social Lab generates income by handling outsourcing needs for local biotech firms.
